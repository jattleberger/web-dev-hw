The internet ships binary information, or 1 and 0s which are bits. There are 8 bits in a byte, 1024 bytes in a kilobyte, and 1024 kilobytes in a megabyte. This information is transmitted through electricity, light, wires, and radio waves.
An IP address or Internet Protocol address is specific to each individual device. DNS or Domain Name System are in a way like a phone book for all the IP addresses.
A cookie is a small file that downloads onto someone device and stores information about a particular client or website. This is how a webpage can be tailored for a specific user.
The three basic parts of a URL are the http/protocol, the domain and subdomain (ie. .com. .org, .gov), and the /directory/ "path" to the page or information you're looking for.
